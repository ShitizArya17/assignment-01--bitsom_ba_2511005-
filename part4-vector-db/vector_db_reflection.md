## Vector DB Use Case
A traditional keyword-based search would not be sufficient for this use case. Keyword search relies on exact or partial matches of words, which makes it ineffective when users phrase queries differently from how the content appears in the document. In large legal contracts, important clauses such as termination conditions may be described using varied terminology (e.g., “contract dissolution,” “early exit provisions,” or “cessation terms”). A keyword-based system may miss these relevant sections if the exact keywords are not present, leading to incomplete or inaccurate results. Additionally, keyword search does not understand context, intent, or semantic meaning, which is critical when dealing with complex legal language.

A vector database, on the other hand, enables semantic search by converting both the contract text and user queries into numerical embeddings. These embeddings capture the meaning of the text rather than just the words. When a lawyer पूछs a question in plain English, the system can compare the query embedding with embeddings of different sections of the contract and retrieve the most semantically similar passages, even if the wording differs significantly.

In this system, the contract would first be broken into smaller chunks (e.g., paragraphs or sections), and embeddings would be generated and stored in a vector database. At query time, the system would retrieve the most relevant chunks based on similarity scores. This approach ensures more accurate, context-aware results and significantly improves the usability and efficiency of searching large legal documents.
